Website Evaluation Baltimore Ravens

Eduardo Cardenas

Course: CSIT121 Web Standards

1. What is the URL of the website?

https://www.baltimoreravens.com

2. What is the name of the website?

Baltimore Ravens Official Website

3. Who is the site's target audience?

The target audience includes Baltimore Ravens fans, football enthusiasts, media, and local community members who want updates on team news, schedules, and events.

4. How is the site organized?

The site uses a clear and logical structure with a top navigation bar linking to sections like News, Videos, Team, Schedule, and Tickets. Content is grouped visually using cards and images to highlight recent games and news stories.

5. Which CRAP Design Principle does the site use? Provide at least one example.

Contrast, the site uses the teams purple and gold color palette effectively. White text on dark backgrounds creates strong visual contrast, especially in the header and navigation menus and improving readability.

6. What is the Audit Score according to the Accessibility Checker?

According to Accessibility checker the site scored approximately 35% on the home page. It says there are 13 critical issues which can put the website under risk of accesibility lawsuit. Some of the issues were "Ensure buttons have accessible names", and ensuring links have discernible text. Some other issues as well. It also passed 36 audits and 45 were not applicable.

7. What is the site's effectiveness?

The site is very effective in delivering current information about the team, including player stats, video highlights, and upcoming game details. Users can easily find the latest updates and official announcements.

8. What is the site's efficiency?

The site is efficient and well-structured. The top navigation and search bar allow users to find content quickly. Pages load fast, and interactive elements such as video players and news cards respond smoothly.

9. How is the engagement?

The website is highly engaging. It uses high-quality visuals, videos, and interactive content to keep visitors interested. The consistent team branding and colors make it feel professional and exciting, matching the NFLs style and energy.

10. Recommendation for improvement

The Baltimore Ravens website looks great but there are a few ways it could be more accessible. Improving the color contrast would make the text easier to read and adding alt text to images would help users who rely on screen readers. It would also be helpful to make sure all buttons and menus are labeled correctly and that the site can be used with just a keyboard. Adding captions to videos and keeping the layout a little simpler would make the experience smoother. Including an accessibility statement and a way for visitors to share feedback would show that the team values all of its fans.
